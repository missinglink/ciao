<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px"><b><a href="/wiki/Alexis_Bachelot" title="Alexis Bachelot">Alexis Bachelot</a></b> (1796–1837) was a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_priest" title="Roman Catholic priest" class="mw-redirect">Roman Catholic priest</a> and first <a href="/wiki/Prefecture_Apostolic_of_the_Sandwich_Islands" title="Prefecture Apostolic of the Sandwich Islands">Prefect Apostolic of the Sandwich Islands</a> who led the first permanent Catholic <a href="/wiki/Mission_(Christianity)" title="Mission (Christianity)">mission</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hawaii" title="Kingdom of Hawaii">Kingdom of Hawaii</a>. He arrived in Hawaii in 1827, expecting the approval of King <a href="/wiki/Kamehameha_II" title="Kamehameha II">Kamehameha&#160;II</a>. He learned upon arrival that the king had died and that the new government was hostile towards Catholic missionaries. Bachelot nevertheless converted a small group of Hawaiians and quietly ministered to them for four years before being deported on the orders of <a href="/wiki/Ka%CA%BBahumanu" title="Kaʻahumanu">Kaʻahumanu</a>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Kuhina_Nui" title="Kuhina Nui">Kuhina Nui</a></i> of Hawaii. Bachelot traveled to California, where he served as an assistant minister. In 1837, having learned of Kaʻahumanu's death and King <a href="/wiki/Kamehameha_III" title="Kamehameha III">Kamehameha&#160;III</a>'s willingness to allow Catholic priests, Bachelot returned to Hawaii, but found that Kamehameha&#160;III had changed his mind. Bachelot was removed from the island and confined to a ship for several months. He was freed after the French and British navies imposed a blockade on Honolulu harbor. He later secured passage on a ship to <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a>, but died en route. His treatment prompted the government of France to dispatch a frigate to Hawaii, an intervention that led to the emancipation of Catholics there.
